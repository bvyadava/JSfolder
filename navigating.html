Chapter 1

The HTML nav element represents a secion of a page whose purpose is to provide navigation links, either within the current document or to other documents. Common examples of navigation sections are menus, tables of contents, and indexes.

Chapter 2

In this example, a nav block is used to contain an unordered list ul of links. With appropriate CSS, this can be presented as a sidebar, navigation bar, or drop-down menu.
